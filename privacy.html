Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 28, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Diligent Developers ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects information when you use our property and construction management platform at diligentdevelopers.app (the "Service"). It applies to everyone who uses the Service, including our staff, subcontractors, and customers.

01 Who We Are

Diligent Developers is a home construction and property development company. We operate an internal and customer-facing platform used to manage projects, schedules, inspections, punch lists, subcontractors, customer upgrade selections, and related construction work. If you have questions about this policy or your information, contact us using the details in the Contact Us section below.

02 Information We Collect

We collect information that you provide directly, information created as you use the Service, and limited information from third-party services you choose to connect.

Information you or your company provide

  • Account and contact details — name, email address, phone number, role, and login credentials.
  • Customer information — names, home and project addresses, contact numbers, email addresses, and upgrade or design selections.
  • Subcontractor information — company name, owner and manager names, trade, phone, email, address, and access codes.
  • Project and construction data — project details, addresses, units, schedules, construction phases, inspections, fix lists, punch lists, house specifications, photos, notes, and signatures.
  • Communications — messages, comments, notes, and notifications created within the Service.

Information created through use

  • Activity data — actions taken in the platform, such as tasks created, items checked off, status changes, and records edited or deleted.
  • Device and technical data — basic information your browser or device provides, such as browser type and general settings, used to keep the Service working and secure.

Information from connected services

  • Google Calendar — if you choose to connect Google Calendar, we receive a temporary access token that lets the Service add events to your own calendar. See the Google Calendar & Google API Services section for full detail.

03 How We Use Information

We use the information we collect to operate and improve the Service, specifically to:

  • Provide, maintain, and secure the platform and your account.
  • Manage projects, schedules, inspections, punch lists, and customer upgrade selections.
  • Let authorized users coordinate work, assign tasks, and communicate.
  • Give subcontractors and customers access to the information relevant to them.
  • Add schedule items and tasks to your Google Calendar when you connect it.
  • Send notifications and updates related to your work or project.
  • Diagnose problems, prevent misuse, and keep data accurate and available.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use it for advertising.

04 Google Calendar & Google API Services

Limited Use disclosure

Our use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We only use Google Calendar access to provide and improve the calendar features you choose to use, and for no other purpose.

Connecting Google Calendar is optional. When you connect it:

  • You sign in through Google's own consent screen and grant permission to manage events on your calendar (the calendar.events scope).
  • The Service receives a temporary access token that lasts only for your current session. We do not receive or store your Google password.
  • We use this access solely to add or update calendar events for schedule items and tasks that are relevant to you.
  • Events are added to your own primary Google Calendar. Each person who connects only affects their own calendar.

We do not read your existing calendar events for any purpose other than creating or updating the events described above, we do not share Google user data with third parties, we do not use it for advertising, and we do not use it to train any AI or machine-learning models. You can disconnect at any time within the app, and you can also remove the Service's access from your Google Account permissions page.

05 How Information Is Stored & Secured

The Service stores data using Google Firebase (Cloud Firestore), a hosted database service provided by Google. Data is transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS) and stored on Google's infrastructure. Some data may also be cached locally on your device so the app works quickly and offline.

We take reasonable measures to protect information, including access controls and role-based visibility so people see the information appropriate to their role. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

We keep information for as long as it is needed to provide the Service and for legitimate business or legal purposes, such as maintaining project and construction records. When information is deleted within the app, it is removed from our active database. Backups or cached copies may persist for a limited time before being overwritten.

Customer project records may include household details provided by the adult customer; these are treated as project information, not as information collected directly from children.
